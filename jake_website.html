Here I want to share more about myself and some of the projects I've worked on in the past.

I am a PhD student at UT Austin and a memeber of the Center for Nonlinear Dynamics in the Department of Physics. As part of the Alvarado Lab, I build simple robots to help uncover and understand the underlying physical design principles of biological systems.

Entering grad school, I had very limited robotics knowledge and basically had to 'figure-it-out' as I went. One thing I've notice in trying to build simple robots is that, sure there's material out there, but not many well written, complete top-to-bottom tutorials. In addition to providing my professional experience here, I want to address this gap and share complete tutorials on how to build simple machines. I plan to include lists of necessary hardware, links to code, wiring diagrams, and theory and explanation where I can offer it.
